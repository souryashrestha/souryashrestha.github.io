I am a Research Associate at Johns Hopkins School of Public Heath in Baltimore, USA. My research, very broadly, focuses on the dynamics of infectious diseases. I use mathematical and computational models, along with data and statistical tools to understand the roles of potential drivers in the transmission of infectious diseases, and how to effectively and efficaciously control the transmission of various infectious pathogens, such as dengue virus, pneumococcus and mycobacterium tuberculosis. Some of my current projects include: (i) Transmission and control of TB in the US; (ii) Targeted distribution of adult vaccines of TB; (iii) HIV-TB interventions.

I recieved my PhD. in Applied and Interdisciplinary Mathematics from University of Michigan in Ann Arbor. I received postdoctoral training in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, and the Center for Studies of Complex Systems at University of Michigan, with Drs Pejman Rohani and Aaron A. King, with Dr. David Dowdy at Johns Hopkins School of Public Health.
